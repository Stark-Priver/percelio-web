## Before Pacelio

Juma spent 3 years waiting at the Soweto motorcycle stage. His experience was typical of informal couriers in Tanzania:

- **Income:** Unpredictable — TZS 0 to TZS 30,000 per day
- **Waiting time:** 2–5 hours per day sitting at the stage
- **Haggling:** Every delivery required price negotiation
- **No tracking:** Customers called repeatedly to check status
- **No proof:** Disputes were common — "I delivered it" vs "You didn't"
- **No growth:** Same income, same challenges, year after year

*"The waiting was the hardest part. You'd sit there watching other riders get jobs, wondering if today would be a good day or a zero day."*

## Tips for New Couriers (from Juma)

1. **Accept what you can handle** — reliability builds your rating
2. **Use the GPS** — even if you know the route, it's optimized for multiple deliveries
3. **Communicate with customers** — a quick message goes a long way
4. **Keep your bike clean** — first impressions matter
5. **Work peak hours** — 7–9 AM, 12–2 PM, 5–7 PM for maximum jobs
