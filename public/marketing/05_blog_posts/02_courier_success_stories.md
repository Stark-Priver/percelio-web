## From Waiting at the Stage to Earning TZS 35,000/Day: How Pacelio is Changing Lives in Mbeya

Juma K. used to start his day the same way every morning for three years.

He'd ride his motorcycle to the stage in Soweto, park alongside 20 other riders, and wait. Sometimes a customer would walk over looking for a delivery. Most days, they'd negotiate the price. Often, they'd walk away.

On a good day, Juma made TZS 30,000. On a bad day, he made nothing.

"The waiting was the hardest part," Juma says. "You'd sit there for hours, watching your colleagues get jobs while you waited. And when you did get a delivery, there was no guarantee you'd get paid."

**A New Way to Work**

Everything changed when Juma heard about Pacelio from a fellow rider.

"At first I was skeptical. Another app? But my friend showed me how it works. Jobs come to your phone. You see the pickup location, drop-off, and price before you accept. The GPS shows you the best route."

Juma signed up, completed verification, and started accepting deliveries.

"On my first day, I got 6 jobs. I earned TZS 22,000. I couldn't believe it. No waiting. No haggling. Just deliveries."

**Life as a Pacelio Courier**

Today, Juma is one of the top-rated couriers on Pacelio with a 4.9-star rating from 300+ deliveries.

His typical day:
- **6:30 AM:** First job alert — breakfast deliveries
- **9:00 AM – 12:00 PM:** Business deliveries (documents, goods, supplies)
- **12:00 PM – 2:00 PM:** Lunch rush — restaurant deliveries
- **2:00 PM – 6:00 PM:** Afternoon deliveries
- **Daily average:** 8–10 deliveries
- **Daily earnings:** TZS 30,000 – 45,000

"The best part is the flexibility," Juma explains. "If I need to take my son to school, I decline jobs. If I want to work late, the jobs keep coming. I'm in control."

**Tips for New Couriers**

Juma's advice for riders considering Pacelio:

1. **Be reliable** — accept jobs you can complete, show up on time
2. **Use the GPS** — it saves fuel and time
3. **Be professional** — greet customers, handle packages carefully
4. **Build your rating** — higher ratings mean more and better jobs
5. **Plan your day** — morning breakfast runs, midday business deliveries, evening food

**The Bigger Picture**

Juma's story is not unique. Across Mbeya, hundreds of couriers are finding consistent work through Pacelio. For many, it's the first time they've had a reliable income without depending on middlemen or luck.

"Pacelio gave me dignity," Juma says quietly. "I'm not waiting anymore. I'm working."
