## How AI is Optimizing Last-Mile Delivery in Tanzania

Last-mile delivery is the most expensive and complex part of any logistics operation. It accounts for 53% of total shipping costs globally. In East Africa, where road infrastructure is limited and addresses are often descriptive ("near the big tree after the petrol station"), the challenge is even greater.

At Pacelio, we're using AI to solve this — not with expensive autonomous vehicles or drones, but with smart algorithms that work within Tanzania's real-world constraints.

**The Challenge**

When a business books a delivery on Pacelio, our system needs to answer several questions in real time:

1. Which couriers are available near the pickup location?
2. Who is the best match for this specific delivery?
3. What's the optimal route?
4. How long will it take?
5. What should it cost?

These decisions happen in milliseconds. The difference between a 25-minute delivery and a 50-minute delivery often comes down to how well these questions are answered.

**How Our AI Works**

**1. Smart Matching**
When a delivery is booked, our algorithm considers:
- **Distance** — courier proximity to pickup
- **Capacity** — does the courier have other active deliveries?
- **Rating** — higher-rated couriers get priority
- **History** — has this courier delivered to this area before?
- **Vehicle type** — motorcycle vs car vs truck

The result: the right courier for each specific job, not just whoever is closest.

**2. Route Optimization**
Once a courier accepts, our system calculates the most efficient route considering:
- **Traffic patterns** — time-of-day road congestion
- **Road conditions** — paved vs unpaved (we're building this data layer)
- **Multiple stops** — if the courier has multiple deliveries
- **Fuel efficiency** — shortest distance isn't always fastest

Early results show a 15–20% reduction in delivery time and fuel costs for couriers using optimized routes.

**3. Dynamic Pricing**
Our pricing algorithm adjusts based on:
- **Supply and demand** — more couriers available = lower prices
- **Distance and weight** — base calculation
- **Urgency** — express delivery options
- **Weather and time of day** — premium during peak hours

**Real Results in Mbeya**

Since launching our AI routing in July 2026:
- Average delivery time dropped from 38 to 27 minutes
- Courier fuel costs reduced by 18%
- Customer satisfaction (rating) increased from 4.5 to 4.8
- Same-day delivery success rate: 96%

**The Future**

We're training our AI on every delivery to improve continuously. Future features include:
- Predictive demand (knowing where deliveries will be needed before they're booked)
- AI-powered address resolution (converting descriptive addresses to precise locations)
- Dynamic courier positioning (suggesting where couriers should wait during slow periods)

**The Bottom Line**

AI in logistics doesn't have to mean drones and robots. In Tanzania, it means smarter matching, better routes, and fairer prices. It means a courier saving TZS 5,000 on fuel per week. It means a customer getting their package 10 minutes faster. It means a business serving more customers with less effort.

That's the kind of AI that matters.
