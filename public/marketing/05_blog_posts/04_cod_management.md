## Cash on Delivery in the Digital Age: How Pacelio Solves the COD Problem

Cash on Delivery (COD) is the backbone of East African e-commerce.

Despite the rise of mobile money, 60% of deliveries in Tanzania are still paid in cash at the point of delivery. For many customers — especially those without smartphones or bank accounts — COD is the only option.

But for businesses, COD is a nightmare.

**The COD Problem**

Running a COD operation means:

- 📓 **Manual tracking** — every delivery requires writing down the amount, the order, and whether payment was collected
- 💵 **Cash handling** — couriers collect cash, which creates security risks and reconciliation headaches
- 🔄 **Reconciliation** — matching payments to orders is time-consuming and error-prone
- ❌ **Disputes** — customers claim they paid, couriers say they didn't, and there's no way to verify
- 📉 **Cash flow** — money is tied up in the delivery process, making it hard to manage finances

For a small business owner, this can mean hours of administrative work every week — time that could be spent growing the business.

**How Pacelio Automates COD**

We built COD management directly into the Pacelio platform. Here's how it works:

**Step 1: Customer selects COD at checkout**
When a customer places an order, they choose "Cash on Delivery" as their payment method.

**Step 2: Courier collects payment at delivery**
The courier arrives, scans the QR code for proof of delivery, and collects the cash from the customer.

**Step 3: Payment is recorded automatically**
The moment the courier confirms delivery, the COD amount is recorded in the system — linked to the specific order, customer, and courier.

**Step 4: Reconciliation happens in real time**
Business owners can see exactly which COD payments have been collected, which are pending, and which have been remitted.

**Step 5: Automated payouts**
COD collections are reconciled against courier payouts automatically. No more chasing payments.
