## Why East Africa Needs a Digital Logistics Revolution

The East African Community is at a crossroads. Cross-border trade has grown 40% since 2020. E-commerce is exploding. Urbanization is accelerating. Mobile money is in the hands of 70% of adults.

Yet if you walk into any small business in Mbeya, Arusha, or Mwanza and ask how they handle deliveries, you'll hear the same story:

*"We call a rider. Hope they show up. Hope the package arrives. Hope we get paid."*

This isn't logistics. It's anxiety.

**The Size of the Gap**

East Africa's courier and logistics market is worth TZS 47.8 trillion (~$19 billion). It's growing at 15% annually. Intra-regional trade is booming thanks to the African Continental Free Trade Area.

But 65% of this market has zero digital solution.

What does that look like in practice?

- Businesses coordinate deliveries through **phone calls** — an average of 3–5 calls per delivery
- **Cash on delivery** is managed with **notebooks** — prone to errors, disputes, and theft
- There is **no real-time tracking** — parcels disappear for hours or days
- **Proof of delivery** is a handshake — or an argument
- **Last-mile delivery costs** remain the highest barrier for small businesses

**Why This Matters**

For East Africa's economy to reach its potential, logistics cannot be a bottleneck. SMEs are the backbone of the region — they account for 90% of businesses and employ 70% of the workforce. But without reliable, affordable delivery, they cannot compete.

A restaurant in Mbeya should be able to deliver to any ward. A boutique in Soweto should serve customers across the city. A hardware store in Mabatini should ship to construction sites in Iyunga.

These are not luxury features. They are basic requirements for a functioning digital economy.
