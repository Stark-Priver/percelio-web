## PACELIO LAUNCHES DIGITAL DELIVERY PLATFORM IN MBEYA, TANZANIA

*Multi-carrier marketplace brings real-time tracking, QR-secured handoffs, and automated COD management to East African logistics*

**MBEYA, TANZANIA** — Pacelio, a multi-carrier courier and logistics platform built by Tanzanian developers Privatus Cosmas and Peter Ntonda, today announced its official launch in Mbeya. The platform connects businesses, courier agents, logistics companies, and individual users through a single digital marketplace.

Pacelio addresses a critical gap in East African logistics: 65% of the region's TZS 47.8 trillion courier market operates without digital solutions. Businesses currently coordinate deliveries through phone calls, manage cash-on-delivery with handwritten records, and lack real-time tracking or proof of delivery.

"We experienced this problem ourselves," said Privatus Cosmas, Co-Founder of Pacelio. "Ordering goods across Mbeya meant coordinating multiple phone calls, waiting hours for a rider, and never knowing when your package would arrive. We built Pacelio to fix this."

The platform offers:
- **Multi-carrier rate comparison** — real-time pricing across available couriers
- **Live GPS tracking** — shareable tracking links for every delivery
- **QR code handoffs** — tamper-proof proof of delivery
- **AI route optimization** — smart dispatch reduces delivery time by 30%
- **Automated COD management** — end-to-end reconciliation
- **In-app chat** — direct communication between senders and couriers

Pacelio launches with 50+ couriers and 120+ partner businesses across all 20 wards of Mbeya city. The platform is available in English and Swahili, reflecting the team's commitment to accessibility.

"Pacelio is built by Tanzanians for East Africa," said Peter Ntonda, Co-Founder. "We understand the local market: mobile money is how people pay, Swahili is how they communicate, and reliability is non-negotiable. Our platform was designed for these realities."

The launch follows a 6-month development period and beta testing phase. Pacelio plans to expand to Dar es Salaam, Arusha, Mwanza, and Dodoma by the end of 2026, with cross-border operations to Kenya and Uganda targeted for 2027.

Pacelio is backed by IncriprTech, a technology startup based in Mbeya.
